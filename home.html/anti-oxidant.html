ANTI-OXIDANT

An antioxidant is a molecule that inhibits the oxidation of other molecules. Oxidation is a chemical reaction that can produce free radicals, leading to chain reactions that may damage cells. Antioxidants terminate these chain reactions by removing free radical intermediates and inhibit other oxidation reactions.

They are often touted for their potential health benefits, as they may help protect against diseases such as cancer, heart disease, and other chronic conditions by neutralizing free radicals and reducing oxidative stress in the body. Antioxidants can be found naturally in certain foods like fruits, vegetables, nuts, and whole grains, as well as in dietary supplements. Some common antioxidants include vitamins C and E, beta-carotene, selenium, and flavonoids.
